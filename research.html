3 Current themes

Below is a sample of research themes I am currently working on. They are more focused than the broad Research Interests sketched above, and of course change a little bit as time goes by.

  • Hydrologic modelling based on simple dynamical systems identified from streamflow and precipitation data; inclusion of physically-based evapotranspiration estimates.
  • Closing the water and energy budgets simultaneously at the watershed scale.
  • Developing and improving lake evaporation models and basin-wide evapotranspiration with the help of remotely-sensed surface temperatures.
  • Dynamic equations and models for the spectral tensor \(\Phi_{i,j}(\vec{k},t)\) in turbulence.
  • RANS (Reynolds-Averaged Navier-Stokes) models running in domains at the meso-beta and meso-gamma scales and incorporating topographical and surface heterogeneity effects.
  • The applicability, or not, of Monin-Obukhov Similarity Theory (MOST) (and extension or alternatives) over the Amazon forest.
  • Corrections for Atmospheric Turbulence Measurements: sensor separation, path averaging and time response, among others.
  • Time-series analysis tools. Spectra and ogives.
  • Use of the length scale \(\ell_\epsilon = u_*^3/\epsilon\) (\(\epsilon\) is the rate of dissipation of turbulence kinetic energy) to normalize turbulence statistics in the Atmospheric Surface Layer.
  • CO2 fluxes to and from Itaipu Lake, and more generally greenhouse gas fluxes to and from lakes.
  • The Hurst Phenomenon in Turbulence, and the existence, or not, of the Integral Scale.
  • Rayleigh-BÃ©nard convection and similarity between temperature and a passive scalar.
  • The nonlinear Boussinesq differential equation, its many incarnations, and the search for new analytical solutions.

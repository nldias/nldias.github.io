3 Current themes

Below is a sample of research themes I am currently working on. They are more focused than the broad Research Interests sketched above, and of course change a little bit as time goes by.

  • Episodes of Ozone increase caused by convective storms in the Amazon region.
  • The applicability (and hopefully extension) of Monin-Obukhov Similarity Theory (MOST) to the roughness sublayer over the Amazon forest.
  • Surface Renewal Theory over water.
  • Use of the length scale \(\ell_\epsilon = u_*^3/\epsilon\) (\(\epsilon\) is the rate of dissipation of turbulence kinetic energy) to normalize turbulence statistics in the Atmospheric Surface Layer.
  • CO2 fluxes to and from Itaipu Lake, and more generally greenhouse gas fluxes to and from lakes.
  • The Hurst Phenomenon in Turbulence, and the existence, or not, of the Integral Scale.
  • BÃ©nard-Rayleigh convection and similarity between temperature and a passive scalar.
  • The nonlinear Boussinesq differential equation, its many incarnations, and the search for new analytical solutions.
  • Better estimates of wind velocity at 50 m from surface data and reanalysis (I am currently looking at CFSR (http://cfs.ncep.noaa.gov/cfsr/)).
  • Time-series analysis tools. Spectra and ogives.
  • Air-quality (Ozone) diagnostics for the City of Curitiba, PR, Brazil.
